Chimera — How the System Reasons About Counter-Strike 2

A world model learns the game by predicting it; a language bridge verbalizes that understanding; self-play sharpens the reasoning.

One idea: don't make a language model see the game — make a small model predict it, then translate its understanding into words. Understanding and language are separate skills, handled by separate specialists.

World Model
causal transformer · ~19M · d=512
Per-frame: 11 tokens (10 players + global). Attends across players (relational) & time (causal). Objective: predict the state ~1 s ahead.
why predict, not caption: the future is a consequence — you must model dynamics, you can't paraphrase your way to it.
→
The Latent
11 × 512 per frame
The model's understanding of the moment — an internal vector, not human-readable. Everything else reads from this.
judged by probe transfer, not loss.
Heads on the latent
Distributional head — predicts a distribution over 97 movement classes (not one point), so it captures "stay vs peek" instead of averaging into blur. 16 samples = a planning group.
Value head — P(CT wins) from the latent. survives 8 s of imagined rollout (AUC ≈ 0.82) → usable as an RL reward.
why a distribution: a regression head mode-averages; the win is representing the set of plausible futures, which also gives the GRPO group.

32 learned "questions" attend to the latent → 32 soft tokens in Qwen's 4096-d space. Translates space (512→4096) & count (→32).

A fluent writer that knows language but not CS2. Reads the 32 soft tokens, writes tactical reasoning.

→
Reasoning Text
natural language
"CT ~32% — T's are stacking apps for an A execute; rotate now."
↩ The faithfulness loop (NLA). The bridge above is only the encoder half of a Natural-Language Autoencoder. A text-only decoder (reverse-resampler) reads only the generated text and tries to rebuild the latent. How well it reconstructs = a label-free faithfulness score — does the text truly render the model's understanding, or hallucinate past it? Two gates: ablate-the-latent (is the latent used?) + recon-fidelity (is it preserved?).

The two specialists

World Model — the analyst

custom · ~19M params · built by us, from scratch

Thinks in 512-number latents. Deep CS2 understanding from predicting millions of frames. Cannot speak a word of English.

input 687-d frame · hidden 512-d · 6 layers

Qwen 3.6 — the writer

pretrained · 35B (A3B MoE) · off-the-shelf

Fluent in language & world knowledge from trillions of tokens. Knows nothing about CS2. Never reads raw ticks — only the bridge's soft tokens.

hidden ~4096-d · 1,800× bigger than the analyst
