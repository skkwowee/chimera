# Anthropic-Research Lineage Brief — Autoencoders-as-Epistemics & J-Space, Mapped to Chimera

Sources actually consulted: transformer-circuits.pub/2026/nla (fetched), transformer-circuits.pub/2026/workspace (fetched); `/home/soone/chimera/docs/bridge-design.md`, `docs/world-model-design.md`, `docs/decisions-ledger.md`, `scripts/train_world_model.py` (6-layer, d=512, 11-token-per-frame; value head reads `h.mean(dim=2)`). Background lineage from training knowledge: Hinton & Salakhutdinov 2006 (AE), Kingma & Welling 2013 (VAE), Bricken et al. 2023 "Towards Monosemanticity" / Templeton et al. 2024 "Scaling Monosemanticity" (SAEs), Ha & Schmidhuber 2018 / Hafner Dreamer / Schrittwieser MuZero (world-model contrast).

## 1. The common principle, stated from first principles

All four variants share one epistemic move: **you cannot trust a description of hidden content unless the description suffices to rebuild the content.** Reconstruction through a bottleneck is a *certificate of information preservation*, and — the part that matters for design — **the type of the bottleneck determines what the certificate is written in**:

| Variant | Bottleneck type | What reconstruction certifies |
|---|---|---|
| Classic AE | low-dim continuous code | the data is compressible to k dims (says nothing about meaning) |
| VAE | code + prior (KL) | the code space is smooth/sampleable — certifies a *generative* handle |
| SAE / dictionary learning | sparse non-negative combo of an overcomplete basis | activations decompose into few atomic directions; FVU keeps the "features" honest (the dictionary can't be a story detached from the content) |
| NLA (2026) | **natural language text** | a human-readable string carries the activation's content; the AR closing the loop is the anti-confabulation check |

The forcing problem in each case is the same: an unconstrained explainer (a probe, a labeler, a caption) can produce fluent output that is *about* the representation without being *determined by* it. The reconstruction term is what couples explanation to content. NLA's own results show the coupling is necessary but not sufficient — even with 0.6–0.8 FVE, verbalizations contain "verifiably false claims about the target model's input context" (confabulation survives the certificate at the margin).

**Where Chimera already sits in this lineage, honestly:** Phase 1 deliberately uses *prediction*, not reconstruction, as its certificate — and that is first-principles correct, not path-dependence. Reconstructing clean symbolic CS2 state is trivial (the parser already gives it to you losslessly); the caption-SFT +0.008 null result was exactly a reconstruction-of-inputs certificate certifying nothing. The future is a *consequence*, not a reformatting — so `P(next|past)` certifies dynamics-relevant information, which is the only information the project cares about. The AE principle enters Chimera only at the **bridge**, where the question genuinely is preservation-through-a-lossy-channel (latent → English).

## 2. NLA → the Chimera bridge: what transfers, what must change

The bridge+reconstructor in `bridge-design.md` §2b is a genuine NLA instantiation, but four structural facts of the CS2 setting force real departures:

**(a) The target model is not homoiconic.** Anthropic's AV and AR are *copies of the target model* — a language model verbalizing a language model's own activations, trained jointly with RL. Chimera's target (19M game transformer) has no language faculty; the verbalizer is a foreign 35B LLM bolted on through a resampler, and the reconstructor is a reverse-Perceiver over re-embedded text. Consequence: the NLA's "expressivity problem" (AV infers beyond the activation) is *worse* here — Qwen has an enormous CS2/esports prior the 19M latent never contained. This is precisely the embellishment risk the recon gate exists to catch, and it is why ablate-the-latent alone (uses it?) and recon-fidelity (renders it?) are orthogonal and both required. That two-gate design is a correct adaptation, not cargo cult.

**(b) Joint RL training is the thing Chimera must NOT copy.** Anthropic jointly trains AV+AR because their goal is to obtain *any* interpretable readout; degenerate-code risk is a caveat they carry. Chimera's goal is a *gate* — a number someone will trust before spending pod-hours and making a thesis claim. A jointly-trained decoder co-adapts into a private cipher and the certificate is void. The ledger's rule (reported number always from a separate decoder trained on frozen verbalizer outputs; recon-as-reward gated and guarded by readability + value-head-agreement) is the right inversion of the NLA recipe, and the reason is first-principles: **evaluator and optimizee must not share gradients when the output is a certificate.**

**(c) Capacity inverts.** NLA needs hundreds of tokens to reach 0.6–0.8 FVE on a full residual stream. Chimera's pooled-512 `z` is so low-rank (95% variance in 6 dims, per the 2026-06-14 probe) that the certificate is *too cheap* — a weak gate that near-any on-topic text passes. The probe's own conclusion (move the real target to the `[11,512]` per-token grid, keep pooled-512 as floor) is correct; the deeper first-principles point is that **the recon target should be the decision-relevant subspace, not the raw latent** — which is where the second paper comes in (§3).

**(d) The degenerate-training risk changes shape.** NLA's failure mode is the AV reproducing input verbatim. Chimera's verbalizer never sees text input to parrot; its analogue is *template parroting* (text as pure function of prompt/static state — the Era-1 circularity) and *steganography* (latent-encoding gibberish). The existing guards (predictive-output-grounded templates, shuffled/empty-text floor tests, perplexity guard, value-head-agreement on `z_hat`) map one-to-one onto these. One gap: NLA's confabulation finding predicts text that is *thematically right but factually wrong about specifics* will pass cosine-level recon. A per-claim factual audit (does "CT5 rotating" match the actual rollout?) on a sample is cheap and not currently a gate.

**SAEs specifically:** dictionary learning on the world-model latent is the one lineage member that mostly *fails to transfer*. On pooled `z`, ~6 effective dims leaves nothing for an overcomplete sparse basis to decompose. On the per-token grid it could find "game features" (entry-frag posture, rotation, save), but SAE interpretability leans on a cheap labeling loop (max-activating *text* examples read by humans); the CS2 analogue needs the demo viewer per feature — feasible but expensive, and the payoff duplicates what the probe battery + bridge already deliver. Verdict: optional interpretability side-quest, not pipeline.

## 3. J-space / Jacobian lens → the world model: the underexploited transfer

The workspace paper's two load-bearing ideas:

1. **The Jacobian lens**: `J_ℓ = E[∂h_final/∂h_ℓ]` — define "verbalizable/workspace" content *causally*, as the directions whose perturbation moves the model's output, then validate by steering and lens-coordinate patching (54–70% swap success on two-hop intermediates).
2. **Middle-layer workspace**: workspace properties hold only in intermediate layers (~38–92% depth); early layers are noise to the lens, final layers are "motor" (next-token mechanics).

Transfer analysis:

- **The lens transfers directly, with the readout head substituted for the LM head.** The world model has exactly the readouts a Jacobian needs: value head and distributional displacement head. `∂value/∂h_ℓ` and `∂dist-logits/∂h_ℓ` define the **decision-relevant subspace** of the latent — the game analogue of "verbalizable representations." `bridge-design.md` already names "head-Jacobian top-k PCA" but only as recon-target *fallback #2*. First principles says it should be closer to the front: the faithfulness certificate should be written over the subspace that causally drives the model's read of the game, because that is the content the thesis claims the text renders. This upgrades the "pooled-512 is too easy / full grid is too hard" dilemma into a principled middle target.
- **Causal-patching evals transfer and are strictly stronger than ablate-the-latent.** Ablate is all-or-nothing (latent on/off). The workspace paper's swap test suggests a concept-level version: patch the latent along a head-Jacobian direction (e.g., toward "CT winning" or a different top displacement mode) and require the bridge's *text* to track the patch (stated assessment flips; narrated play changes). This tests grounding at the resolution of *content*, not mere *usage*, is CPU-cheap on cached latents at the eval stage, and is not in any current doc. It is the single best new idea this lineage contributes to Chimera.
- **Middle-layer finding transfers as a question, not an answer.** At 6 layers there may be no room for an early/workspace/motor stratification — but the paper's warning stands: the *last* layer is "motor cortex" for the next-state head (committed to frame mechanics), and may be the *wrong* bridge tap. World-model-design open question #4 ("latent read-out point") gets a principled procedure: sweep layers 3–6, pick the one where head-Jacobian directions are (a) causally steerable and (b) persistent across frames (the paper's autocorrelation signature). Currently the bridge taps the final norm output by default — that is path-dependence ("it's what the value head reads"), and it happens to be defensible *only because* the value head co-trained there; say so and verify.
- **What fails to transfer:** the verbal-report and conscious-access framing (the world model never verbalizes; "verbalizable" must be operationalized through the external bridge — a two-system Jacobian `∂text-logits/∂h_wm` is possible but crosses the resampler and is a different, untested object); the single-token concept vocabulary (CS2 concepts are relational and multi-agent — the per-player token factoring helps, but the lens must be taken per-slot); the ~10–25-concept sparsity claim (with ~6 effective pooled dims the "workspace" may be degenerate — finding that it is would itself be a result: the model's decision state is far simpler than the tactical vocabulary humans use, which bounds what any bridge text can faithfully contain).

## 5. What to build differently (concrete deltas, all CPU-light)

1. **Promote head-Jacobian subspace from recon-fallback #2 to the primary recon target** (or the weighting on the grid target). Computable now from `wm_3map_dist_v3m` on cached val frames.
2. **Add a latent-patching grounding eval** (workspace-paper swap test, adapted): perturb `z` along top value-head-Jacobian directions on cached latents; once the bridge exists, require text to track the patch. Design + direction extraction is local; the text half rides the existing milestone pod run.
3. **Layer sweep for the bridge tap** before the QLoRA spend: steerability + cross-frame persistence per layer (3–6). One afternoon on the 4090.
4. **Per-claim factual audit** on a sample of generated text vs actual rollout/future — the NLA confabulation result says cosine-level recon will not catch specific false claims.

## 6. Intellectual-honesty findings

- **"The bridge is the encoder half of an NLA" is a retrofitted framing.** The bridge exists because the thesis needs tactical language out of a latent; NLA was adopted afterward to supply the missing faithfulness leg. The retrofit is *sound* — the gate is justified by the embellishment problem, not by pedigree — but the docs' framing implies more design-from-NLA than occurred. The genuinely NLA-derived elements are the firewall, the controls, and the separate-decoder rule.
- **The final-layer bridge tap is path-dependence** ("it's what the value head reads") until the layer sweep is run. The workspace paper gives concrete reason to suspect it's suboptimal.
- **The workspace paper is currently uncited and unexploited in the repo** — its causal-patching methodology is the highest-value untapped transfer; its middle-layer finding is a cheap check on an acknowledged open question.
- **Phase 1's rejection of the AE principle (prediction over reconstruction) is the project's strongest first-principles move** and should be argued that way in the paper: reconstruction certificates are vacuous when the input is already clean symbolic state; prediction certificates are not. The caption-SFT null (+0.008) is the measured version of this argument.
